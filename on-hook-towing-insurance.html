Common Questions

On-Hook Towing Insurance FAQ

What is on-hook towing insurance?

On-hook (also called in-tow) coverage pays for damage to the customer's vehicle while it's being towed — hooked, loaded, or in transit. It's separate from your truck's own physical damage coverage.

What's the difference between on-hook and garagekeepers?

On-hook covers vehicles while you're towing them; garagekeepers covers vehicles stored in your care at your lot or impound. Most operations need both, and we review both.

How much on-hook coverage do I need?

Enough to cover the value of the most expensive vehicle you'd have in tow at one time. We help you set a limit that fits your operation.

Is on-hook coverage included in my policy?

Not always — it's a common gap. We review your current policy to confirm whether you carry it and at what limit.
